About

Restore

Sleep is precious — and you already know it.

We live in a world that constantly pressures us to sacrifice sleep for the next priority, the next deadline, the next trade-off. But sleep isn’t a luxury. It’s the foundation of how we think, decide, feel, and recover. Without it, everything else suffers.

Restore exists to help you understand your sleep — your patterns, your trends, your habits — and to gently guide you toward better ones. Whether you’re trying to fix a broken sleep schedule or simply curious about what’s going on while you rest, you’ll find the insight you need here.

Better sleep == Better you.

Getting started

Use the icons in the bar below to move between pages:

  • 🛌 Dashboard Overview with recent and lifetime averages, recent nights, and 7-day time and duration charts.
  • ✏️ Log Add or edit a night: bed, sleep, wake, bathroom and alarm times, nap, and WASO.
  • 🟩 Quality Sleep quality history over time.
  • 📅 Daily Day-by-day timeline of bed, sleep, and get-up times.
  • 📊 Graphs Time and duration charts with trend lines across your data.
  • 🔢 Stats Monthly sleep statistics.

Quick actions

The Dashboard suggests shortcuts to log wake, bed, sleep, bathroom, alarms, and naps. Which buttons appear depends on the phase (below), your recent data, and the clock. Tapping an action usually saves to your log; if cloud sync is off, you may see a prompt to enable it in Settings—except nap start/end, which can still be recorded locally until sync is on.

The list refreshes about every 60 seconds while the Dashboard is open.

Which phase runs (order matters)

Quick actions use the same shared time context as the header. Rules are evaluated in this order:

  1. If the header is in dynamic Sleep (see Remaining Wake Time)—bed or fell-asleep logged for the night row, wake not finalized, and the clock is in overnight “limbo” (after average fell-asleep or before average get-up on a typical schedule) or inside the evening sleep window (within 120 minutes before through 240 minutes after your average fell-asleep)—quick actions use the sleep set.
  2. Else if a wake was logged in the last 3 hours (get-up time or quick-action wake flag, on a nearby calendar night row)—use wake.
  3. Else if the clock is within 105 minutes (circular) of your average get-up time—use wake.
  4. Else use wake, sleep, or mid from the same clock windows as the header averages: near get-up → wake; within 120 min before to 240 min after average fell-asleep → sleep; otherwise → mid.
  5. If that header context is unavailable, the app falls back to the same windows using your recent-seven-day averages from the Dashboard slice.

When each action appears

Action Phase Shown when
Wake up wake Always when the wake phase is active.
Log alarm wake Only if your recent nights include alarm data used for averages (first-alarm-to-wake is defined).
Get in bed sleep Bed is not already considered logged for this night (quick-action flag or a non-default bed time within the last 12 hours).
Go to sleep sleep Fell-asleep is not already considered logged for this night.
Sleep in 10 min sleep Neither “both bed and fell-asleep complete” nor fell-asleep alone is already logged as above—i.e. you can still log an upcoming sleep time.
Bathroom break sleep Both bed and fell-asleep are considered logged for this night (flags or matching non-stub times within the last 12 hours).
Start a nap mid Your remaining-wake percentage is at or above the Open threshold (see Adjust thresholds), and you are not in the post-wake 3-hour window or the 180-minute quiet window right after average get-up.

Night row for saves: Bed, sleep, bathroom, and morning actions target the sleep-period row keyed by wake day (evening and late-night edits belong to the morning you will wake into). Wake time and alarm use the same rules, including early-morning correction when yesterday’s row is still waiting for get-up.

Remaining Wake Time

Shows how much time is left in your day before your recommended sleep time. Open, Winding down, and Pre-sleep follow your percent of wake time remaining until average fell-asleep—meant to gently guide your pace, not rush you. You can adjust those boundaries below or in Settings. A fourth phase, Sleep, is dynamic: it appears when you have logged bed or fell-asleep for the night and have not yet logged wake; it is not controlled by the sliders.

The percentage-based phases feel like this:

  • ☀️
    Open time This is the primary portion of your day, with plenty of runway. The feeling is steady, normal, and no urgency.
  • 🌇
    Winding down It is not quite time to sleep, but avoid starting heavy or demanding activities. Caffiene is not recommended from this point.
  • 🛏️
    Pre-sleep Begin preparing for rest. Avoid meals and stimulating activity.
  • 🌙
    Sleep You have logged bed or fell-asleep; wake is not logged yet. The header shows this phase in the evening sleep window or overnight until you log get-up—not from your wake-time percentages.

Adjust thresholds

Percentages are your share of wake time left from your recent 7-day average get-up time to average fell-asleep time—the same window the header clock uses. The times under each slider show roughly when those boundaries fall on a typical day.

Daily flags

How strong is a daily flag?

The flag color is how far that measure drifted from your previous seven logged nights. (Fewer than 7 prior nights in the log will show an insufficient-data message.)

😴 Asleep is only flagged when you fell asleep later than your baseline; falling asleep earlier is not flagged.

⌛ Duration uses the worse of two checks (not added together): shorter than your recent average, or absolute short sleep—under 6 hours (slight), under 5 hours (moderate), under 4 hours (severe)—based on total sleep including naps.

🌅 Wake compares earlier or later than the baseline.

🧩 WASO (wake after sleep onset) counts wake episodes longer than 20 minutes: 1 → slight, 2 → moderate, 3 or more → severe.

On the history calendar, the cell color is the worst of those severities among asleep, duration, and WASO.

Night notes (emoji labels)

From the Log screen you can tag a night with optional emoji “notes” (kids, pet, alcohol, travel, stress, work, caffeine, late meal, illness). They are saved with that night’s row, show next to daily flags on the timeline and dashboard, and do not change scores or charts.
